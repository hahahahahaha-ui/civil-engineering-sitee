What is Structural Engineering?

Structural engineering is a branch of civil engineering that focuses on designing and analyzing structures such as buildings, bridges, and towers to ensure they are strong, stable, and safe.

Click to expand more details

Structural engineers calculate loads and forces acting on structures. They ensure that buildings can resist earthquakes, strong winds, and heavy loads without failing.


Importance of Structural Engineering

Structural engineering is important because it ensures that infrastructures are safe, durable, and reliable for everyday use.

  • Prevents building collapse
  • Supports economic growth
  • Improves disaster resistance

Conclusion

Structural engineering plays a major role in modern society by making sure that all structures are safe and long-lasting.
